Major Arcana( 22 )

The Fool represents new beginnings, taking risks, and living in the moment. It encourages you to let go of your fears and trust in the universe.

Reversed, the Fool represents impulsiveness, carelessness, and lack of direction. It may indicate that you are not thinking things through and that you need to slow down and be more mindful of your actions.

The Magician represents the power of manifestation, creativity, and the ability to turn your ideas into reality. It reminds you to trust in your own abilities and use your talents to achieve your goals.

Reversed, the Magician represents a lack of confidence and a failure to take action. It may indicate that you are not using your talents and abilities to their full potential, and that you need to trust in your own abilities and take action towards your goals.

The High Priestess represents intuition, mystery, and hidden knowledge. She reminds you to trust your instincts and listen to your inner voice.

Reversed, the High Priestess represents a lack of intuition, confusion, and hidden knowledge. It may indicate that you are not trusting your instincts and that you need to take time to listen to your inner voice.

The Empress represents motherhood, nurturing, and abundance. She reminds you to take care of yourself and others and to find happiness in the simple things in life.

Reversed, the Empress represents neglect, lack of nurturing, and lack of abundance. It may indicate that you are not taking care of yourself or others, and that you need to find happiness in the simple things in life.

The Emperor represents authority, stability, and structure. He reminds you to be assertive, take control of your life, and make decisions that will benefit you in the long run.

Reversed, the Emperor represents a lack of authority, instability, and lack of structure. It may indicate that you are not being assertive, that you are not taking control of your life and that you are not making decisions that will benefit you in the long run.

The Hierophant represents tradition, religion, and spiritual guidance. He reminds you to seek wisdom and guidance in your spiritual practices and to find meaning in your beliefs.

Reversed, the Hierophant represents a lack of tradition, religion, and spiritual guidance. It may indicate that you are not seeking wisdom and guidance in your spiritual practices, and that you need to find meaning in your beliefs.

The Lovers represents love, passion, and connection. It reminds you to nurture your relationships and to listen to your heart when making important decisions.

Reversed, the Lovers represent a lack of love, passion, and connection. It may indicate that you are not nurturing your relationships, and that you are not listening to your heart when making important decisions.

The Chariot represents determination, willpower, and success. It reminds you to stay focused and keep moving forward, even in the face of obstacles and challenges.

Reversed, the Chariot represents a lack of determination, willpower, and success. It may indicate that you are not staying focused, that you are giving up easily and that you are not moving forward, even in the face of obstacles and challenges.

Strength represents inner strength, courage, and self-control. It reminds you to stay true to yourself and to trust in your abilities, even when things get tough.

Reversed, Strength represents a lack of inner strength, courage, and self-control. It may indicate that you are not staying true to yourself, and that you are not trusting in your abilities, even when things get tough.

The Hermit represents introspection, solitude, and wisdom. He reminds you to take time for yourself and to seek wisdom through reflection and self-discovery.

Reversed, the Hermit represents a lack of introspection, solitude, and wisdom. It may indicate that you are not taking time for yourself, and that you are not seeking wisdom through reflection and self-discovery.

The Wheel of Fortune represents the ups and downs of life, and reminds you that everything is cyclical and that good and bad times will come and go.

Reversed, the Wheel of Fortune represents a lack of understanding of the ups and downs of life. It may indicate that you are not accepting the cyclical nature of life, and that you are not finding meaning in the good and bad times.

Justice represents balance, fairness, and morality. It reminds you to make decisions based on what is fair and just, and to strive for balance and harmony in your life.

Reversed, Justice represents a lack of balance, fairness, and morality. It may indicate that you are not making decisions based on what is fair and just, and that you are not striving for balance and harmony in your life.

The Hanged Man represents sacrifice, patience, and perspective. He reminds you to be patient, to let go of what you cannot control, and to find meaning in the struggles of life.

Reversed, the Hanged Man represents a lack of sacrifice, patience, and perspective. It may indicate that you are not being patient, that you are not letting go of what you cannot control, and that you are not finding meaning in the struggles of life.

Death represents transformation, change, and rebirth. It reminds you that endings are necessary for new beginnings, and that change is a natural part of life.

Death reversed represents a resistance to change and transformation. It may indicate that you are holding on to the past and not accepting the need for change and rebirth in your life.

Temperance represents balance, self-control, and moderation. It reminds you to find balance in your life, to take things in stride, and to not let your emotions control you.

Reversed, Temperance represents a lack of balance, self-control, and moderation. It may indicate that you are not finding balance in your life, that you are overreacting to situations and that you are letting your emotions control you.

The Devil represents temptation, materialism, and addiction. It reminds you to be aware of your vices, to resist temptation, and to not let material possessions control your life.

Reversed, the Devil represents a release from temptation, materialism, and addiction. It may indicate that you are aware of your vices, that you have overcome them and that you are not letting material possessions control your life.

The Tower represents upheaval, chaos, and sudden change. It reminds you to be prepared for unexpected events, to stay strong in the face of adversity, and to find meaning in the chaos.

Reversed, the Tower represents a resistance to upheaval, chaos, and sudden change. It may indicate that you are not prepared for unexpected events, that you are not staying strong in the face of adversity, and that you are not finding meaning in the chaos.

The Star represents hope, inspiration, and creativity. It reminds you to keep your dreams alive, to find inspiration in the world around you, and to stay optimistic.

Reversed, the Star represents a lack of hope, inspiration, and creativity. It may indicate that you are not keeping your dreams alive, that you are not finding inspiration in the world around you, and that you are feeling pessimistic.

The Moon represents emotions, intuition, and subconscious. It reminds you to trust your instincts, to listen to your inner voice, and to be in tune with your emotions.

Reversed, the Moon represents a lack of emotional awareness, intuition, and subconscious. It may indicate that you are not trusting your instincts, that you are not listening to your inner voice and that you are not in tune with your emotions.

The Sun represents vitality, joy, and success. It reminds you to find happiness in the simple things, to enjoy the present moment, and to radiate positivity and optimism in your daily life.

Reversed, the Sun represents a lack of vitality, joy, and success. It may indicate that you are not finding happiness in the simple things, that you are not enjoying the present moment, and that you are radiating negativity and pessimism in your daily life.

Judgment represents self-reflection, personal growth, and redemption. It reminds you to take responsibility for your actions, to learn from your mistakes, and to strive for personal growth and self-improvement.

Reversed, Judgement represents a lack of self-reflection, personal growth, and redemption. It may indicate that you are not taking responsibility for your actions, that you are not learning from your mistakes, and that you are not striving for personal growth and self-improvement.

The World represents completion, accomplishment, and fulfillment. It reminds you to celebrate your successes, to appreciate the journey, and to find meaning in the completion of your goals and dreams.

Reversed, The World represents incompletion, lack of accomplishment, and unfulfillment. It may indicate that you are not celebrating your successes, that you are not appreciating the journey, and that you are not finding meaning in the completion of your goals and dreams.
